1. set about： 着手，开始
  He set about his task at once with great enthusiasm.
  他立即满腔热情地干了起来。
  She set about preparing supper.
  她张罗着做饭。

2. let down： 使失望
  I haven't done it well. I've let you down.
  我没有做好，我让你失望了。
  You always let me down; I won't trust you again.
  你总是令我失望，我不会再信赖你了。

3. be suspicious of： 对……感到怀疑
  I am suspicious of that boy ― I think he may have stolen something from our shop.
  我很怀疑那个男孩，我想他可能偷了我们商店的东西。

4. do well in (be good at)： 擅长
  be astute in：在……上反应机敏
  She does well in painting.
  她擅长画画。
  He was both astute in politics and steeped in Chinese literature and culture.
  他政治反应机敏，同时还埋头于中国文学和文化。

5. stand up for： 支持
  If it hadn't been for Tom standing up for you, you would have had to leave.
  要不是汤姆为你说话，你恐怕都得走人了。
  We should stand up for the truth.
  我们应该坚持真理。

6. brim with： 注满，满溢
  His laughter brimmed with mirth.
  他的笑声中充满欢乐。
  Tears were brimming in his eyes at that moment.
  那一刻他热泪盈眶。

7. throw caution to the wind： 把警告当作耳旁风，鲁莽行事
  Caution is no longer being thrown to the wind when it comes to color.
  谈及颜色的时候，（他们）就不再把警告当作耳旁风了。

8. a series of ups and downs： 一连串的起起浮浮
  Home mortgages with interest rates that move up and down over time, based on changes in market interest rates.
  住房抵押的利率随着时间上下起浮，是以市场利率的变化为基础的。
  There are a lot of ups and downs in one's life.
  人一生会经历很多次起起落落。

9. steer clear of： 避开，绕开
  I steered clear of being associated with such people as him.
  我避免与像他这样的人交往。

10. turn a blind eye to： 对……视而不见
  It's not easy to turn a blind eye to things you are sick of.
  假装看不见你讨厌的事物，那不太容易。
  The manager turned a blind eye when his staff were late.
  职员迟到时经理装作没看见。

11. set oneself high standards： 对自己高标准要求

12. rely on： 依靠，依赖
  We had thought that we could rely on you, but you let us down.
  我们原来以为可以依靠你们的，但你们却使我们大失所望。
  You must rely on your own efforts.
  你必须依靠自己的努力。

13. speak one's mind： 直言
  Just speak your mind!
  有话直说！

14. go travelling off the beaten tracks： 到人们不常去的地方旅游
  Welcome to off the beaten treks ― Himalayan trekking adventure tours.
  欢迎来到人迹罕至之地――喜玛拉雅跋涉冒险旅行。

15. branch out into new areas： 向新领域扩展
  Soon after it was planted, the rosebush began to branch out.
  蔷薇丛栽下不久就开始抽枝了。
  At the age of 30 he left his family business and branched out on his own.
  他30岁的时候便脱离了家族公司并开始独立经营。

16. be wary of acting： 行事谨慎
  You'd better be wary of those flatterers.
  你最好提防那些溜须拍马的人。

17. in demand： 有需要，销路好
  Oil is in great demand these days.
  目前对石油的需求很大。
  Jane was in frequent demand socially.
  简常被邀请参加社交活动。

18. sort out： 解决（本课中的意思）；挑选出；整理（东西、思想等）
  You need to get your own problems sorted out.
  你需要把自己的问题解决好。
  Sort out the best apples for eating and cook the rest.
  把最好的苹果挑出来吃，其余的煮一煮。
  It takes me some time to sort out my thoughts before I can start writing.
  下笔之前，我得花些时间理清思路。

19. stick to： 坚持（本课中的意思）；粘住，固定在；忠于
  stick to the principle of fair play：坚持公平交易的原则
  keep the biscuits from sticking to the pan：不让烤饼粘锅
  stick to your lover：忠于爱人

20. at ease： 自由自在，安逸
  I was never at ease with him.
  我和他在一起总是感到不自在。
  My mind was at ease knowing that the children were safe.
  听说孩子们都很安全，我才放心。

21. Never one to let the grass grow under your feet.
  没有人会错过大好时机。

22. straight away： 马上
  The DVD will ship straight away, provided your payment goes through successfully.
  只要你的付款顺利，我们将立刻送出DVD。

23. with an addition to： 由于……的增加
  With an addition to the workforce, we can clearly see an increase in labor.
  由于劳动力的增加，我们可以清楚地看到劳动量的增大。

24. rush into： 仓促行动
  rush into a marriage：仓促结婚
  rush into a decision：匆匆作出决定
