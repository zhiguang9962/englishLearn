1. a shop selling gramophone records： 一家卖唱片的商店
  I used to work in a shop selling shoes.
  我过去在一家鞋店工作。
  He knows a good shop selling men's shirts.
  他知道一家不错的男式衬衣店。

2. a middle-aged woman： 一个中年妇女
  the middle-aged spread：中年体型
  the middle-distance race：中长跑
  a middle-sized shirt：一件中号的衬衣
  the Middle Ages：中世纪，中古时代
  the middle class：中产阶级

3. sit on a stool in front of the counter： 坐在柜台前的一只凳子上
  注意 in front of 和 in the front of 的区别：前者是某物的前面，指脱离此物体；
  后者是某物前面的一部分，是依附此物体而言。
  Don't stand in front of me. I can't see anything.
  别站在我前面，我什么都看不见了。
  I sat in the front of the bus to get a good view of the small town.
  我坐在公共汽车的前部，以便饱览小城风光。

4. smile at somebody brightly： 很高兴地向某人微笑
  They smiled at the bad condition and kept going.
  他们不在乎恶劣的条件，还是坚持出发了。
  Fortune has always smiled at her.
  她的命运一向很好。

5. without much hope： 不抱太多希望
  There is not much hope of their being still alive.
  他们仍然活着的希望不大。
  Don't give up; there is a ray of hope.
  别放弃，还有一线希望。
  He left without any hope of her coming back.
  他走了，对她的回来不抱一点希望。

6. I should know it if I heard it, though.
  可是，如果我听到的话，我就能知道。
  Snow is not predicted; we can expect some rain, though.
  不可能下雪，但可能下雨。
  Though I was poor I was happy.
  虽然我很穷却很快乐。

7. play me a few records： 给我放几张唱片
  Thanks very much for playing me such wonderful music.
  非常感谢为我播放如此美妙的音乐。
  I could only play you parts of each chapter.
  我只能每一章节给你放一部分。

8. pick out： 挑选
  She was picked out from thousands of applicants for the job.
  从数千申请人中挑选出她来做那份工作。
  I picked out the reddest apple.
  我挑了一个最红的苹果。

9. She settled herself more comfortably on her stool.
  她调整了一下位置，很舒服地坐在凳子上。
  The sparrow settled on the wire.
  麻雀停在电线上。
  It was so noisy outside that I couldn't settle myself down to work.
  外面那么吵，我都无法安下心来工作了。

10. in stock： 备有，库存
  floor stock：基本储备
  out of stock：没有现货，脱销
  averaging stock：平均库存

11. National Anthem： 国歌
  national policy：国家政策
  national flag：国旗
  national origin：民族起源
  national debt：国债

12. in tune： 合调，入调
  The guitar is out of tune.
  这把吉他音调不正。
  The violin is not quite in tune with the piano.
  这小提琴跟钢琴的调子不太和谐。

13. get into a complete muddle： 完全陷入一片混乱中
  I am in a muddle; I even forget today is Saturday ― I needn't go to work.
  我糊里糊涂，甚至忘了今天是星期六――我不用上班。
  You always make a muddle of everything.
  你总是把所有的事情搞得一团糟。

14. be to do something： 开始做某事，应该做某事
  It was not to be.
  不应有此事。
  I am to visit Mrs. Green tomorrow.
  我明天要去拜访格林太太。

15. look quite depressed： 看上去非常沮丧
  She was depressed because she had not passed her examinations.
  她很沮丧，因为她没有通过考试。
  He seemed quite depressed; nobody knew why.
  他似乎很沮丧，没有人知道为什么。

16. as if： 好像
  It seemed as if they would lose the game.
  看起来他们要输掉比赛了。
  She behaved as if nothing had happened.
  她装成若无其事的样子。

17. lack of： 缺乏，短缺
  There is no lack of food.
  食物不缺。
  The lack of experiences is the cause of her failure.
  缺乏经验是她失败的原因。

18. if you see what I mean： 如果你明白我的意思
  I see your point.
  我明白你的意思。
  What are you talking about on earth? I can't see your meaning at all.
  你到底在说什么？我一点也不明白你的意思。

19. enough： adj. & adv. 足够
  enough 作形容词修饰名词时，放在名词前后均可；它作副词修饰形容词或副词时，
  须放在所修饰词的后面。
  Two men are quite enough for the job.
  有两个男人干这件事就够了。
  There is enough food for everybody.
  有足够的食物供大家吃。
  It is warm enough to swim.
  天气足够暖和，可以去游泳。

20. I suppose you are new to the job.
  我想你是新手吧!
  I suppose it will snow.
  我认为将会下雪。
  I suppose you are right.
  我想你说得对。
  I was new to the town. I had never been there before.
  我对这个城镇不熟悉，我从前没去过那儿。

21. Casualty Department： 创伤科，急诊室
  in-patient department：住院部
  financial department：财务处
  issue department：发行部
  personnel department：人事处

22. take off： 脱下
  He felt a little hot and took his coat off.
  他觉得有点热，便脱下了外套。
  Please take off your dirty shoes before entering the room.
  在进入房间之前请把你的脏鞋脱下来。
 
23. lie down： 躺下
  注意 lie 作“躺，卧”讲时的过去式和过去分词为 lay，lain；它作“说谎”讲时则为 lied。
  She lay down on the sofa.
  她躺在沙发上。
  Tom was lying in the shade of the tree.
  汤姆正躺在树荫下。
  I found him lying under a tree to sleep.
  我发现他正躺在树下睡觉。
  You have lied yourself into trouble.
  你的谎言给自己带来麻烦。

24. hold up your right arm： 举起你的右臂
  Don't move. Hold up your hands.
  不许动。举起手来。
 
25. Does this hurt?
  这样疼吗？
  My right arm hurts.
  我的右臂疼。
  Nothing hurts more than a bad tooth.
  没有比牙痛更痛的了。

26. for example： 例如
  You can wear something simple; for example, a skirt and blouse.
  你可以穿简单的衣服，比如说，一条裙子和一件衬衫。
  I know many women who have a career and a family ― Alice for example.
  我知道有许多妇女能家庭事业兼顾――艾丽丝就是其中之一。
  Please give me an example.
  请给我举个例子。
