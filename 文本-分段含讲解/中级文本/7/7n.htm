1. Selfridges： 伦敦的一家大百货公司，位于牛津街上（Oxford Street）
  Liberty's：伦敦的一家大商店
  Harrods：伦敦的一家高级百货商店

2. a smart store： 一个漂亮的商店
  a smart price：挺贵的价格
  a smart few people：相当多的人
  a smart saying：俏皮话

3. go window-shopping： 到商店里欣赏商品（并不想买）

4. on display： 被展示，被陈列
  A collection of photographs was on display in the hall.
  大厅里展出了一辑照片。

5. the sales： 大拍卖, 减价出售
  I always buy goods in the sales.
  我总是在减价期间购物。
  The shop held an end-of-season sale, which lasted a week.
  商店举办了季末大减价，活动持续了一个星期。

6. be worth it： 值得花费时间或精力，有必要
  The new car costs a lot of money, but it's certainly worth it.
  买这辆新汽车花了很多钱，但确实物有所值。
  It's such a small point that it's hardly worth being troubled about.
  这个问题很小，不值得研究。

7. reflect v. 想到（后接从句）
  On my way home I reflected how selfish I had been to act like that.
  在回家路上我想，我那样做是多么自私。
  She reflected that he was a wealthy man anyway.
  她寻思，不管怎样，他是个有钱人。

8. a most disagreeable experience： 令人非常不愉快的经历
  experience作名词当“经历”讲时可数，当“经验”讲时不可数。
  I had many interesting experiences while traveling in Australia.
  我在澳大利亚旅行时，有许多有趣的经历。
  Do you have much experience of teaching?
  你教学经验丰富吗？

9. tread on： 踏，踩
  I am sorry that I trod on your toes.
  我踩了你的脚, 对不起。
  Don't tread on the crops.
  不要践踏庄稼。

10. poke with： （以手指, 肘等）杵，碰；（用手指、尖物）刺，戳，拨弄
  He poked me in the ribs with his elbow.
  他用肘杵我的肋骨。
  I poked the fire with a stick.
  我用一根棍子拨火。

11. knock over： 撞倒，击倒（作此意时可knock down互换）
  He came hurrying in and almost knocked me down (over).
  他匆忙地进来，差点把我撞倒。
  With one blow he knocked his opponent down (over).
  他一拳把对手打倒。

12. in one's haste： 匆忙，急促
  In my haste I forgot my watch.
  我在匆忙中忘了我的手表。
  Marry in haste and repent at leisure.
  [谚]结婚太急，后悔莫及。

13. by chance： 偶然，意外地
  I met him quite by chance.
  我遇见他完全是偶然的。
  Would you by any chance have change for $5?
  你能换开5美元吗？
  It happened by some chance.
  不知为什么这事就发生了。

14. on sale： 出售，上市
  The new model is not on sale in the shops.
  这种新款式尚未在商店出售。
  I'm sorry this CD is not for sale.
  很抱歉，这张CD是非卖品。

15. be bound to： 必定，一定
  All those who try to falsify history are bound to end up as criminals in history.
  一切伪造历史的人必然会成为历史的罪人。
  The weather is bound to get better tomorrow.
  明天天气一定会变好。

16. be in an especially cheerful mood： 心情特别高兴
  I'm always in a good mood on Fridays.
  我每周五情绪都很好。
  He's in a mood now, and you'd better not talk with him.
  他正在闹情绪，你最好别和他说话。
