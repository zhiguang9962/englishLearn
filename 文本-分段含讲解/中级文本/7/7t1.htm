Gretel and Mrs. Clark went shopping in the centre of London yesterday.
    "I'd like to know which store you like best in London, Mrs. Clark," Gretel said.
    "Now that's a difficult question," Mrs. Clark replied. "I just haven't any idea which store I like best. There are so many huge stores in London. I suppose Selfridges must be the biggest. There's so much variety there. I can usually find what I'm looking for. Then there's Liberty's. It's such a lovely building. Harrods is very famous, too. It's such a smart store. I love all the big stores in London because you can walk round and nobody bothers you. Nobody tries to sell you anything ¡ª unless you want to buy something. Sometimes I go window-shopping, or just wander round a store and look at the things on display. The big stores are one of the sights of London. I went to the sales in January. That was one of the sights of London, too! My goodness! The crowds! But it was worth it. I bought some lovely things very cheaply. It was good fun, but very exhausting."
