1. once upon a time： 从前（一般用于童话开头）
  Once upon a time there was a beautiful princess.
  从前有个美丽的公主。

2. make it a rule to do something： 把做某事定为常规
  it 在这里是形式宾语，真正的宾语是动词不定式，a rule 作为宾语补足语。
  I find it right to do so.
  我发现这样做是对的。
  He made it no use trying.
  他试也没有用。
  I make a rule of getting up early.
  我有早起的习惯。

3. in that order： 按那个顺序
  Please rearrange the following sentences in the proper order.
  请按照正确的顺序重新排列以下句子。
  He made his affairs out of order.
  他把事情弄得乱七八糟。

4. the day before the imperial interviews： 皇帝接见的前一天
  I went over the lesson again on the day before examination.
  在考试前一天我把功课又复习了一遍。
  Thanks very much for your interview.
  非常感谢你的接见。
  I will have an interview with the reporters at three o'clock.
  我三点钟要接见记者。

5. get oneself enlisted in the army： 使自己参军
  He got himself promoted by all means.
  他千方百计地使自己升了职。
  He enlisted for military service in 1978.
  他于1978年入伍。

6. on tenterhooks： 提心吊胆
  She was kept on tenterhooks for hours while the judges were deciding the winners.
  她在等待裁判员裁决出优胜者，几个小时如坐针毡。

7. the shortest possible answers to the imperial questions：
  对皇帝的问题做出的最简短回答
  What's your answer to that?
  你对那件事的答案是什么？
  Did you find the key to the lock?
  你找到那把锁的钥匙了吗？
 
8. befit： vt. 适合
  His clothes doesn't befit the occasion.
  他的服装不适合那种场合。
  You should wear the formal attire that befits the occasion.
  你应该穿适于这个场合的正式装束。

9. be seated on the throne： 坐在御座上
  I was seated in a chair comfortably.
  我舒服地坐在椅子上。
  He seated himself at a writing table.
  他在写字台前坐下。

10. far from being nervous： 一点都不紧张
  Far from enjoying singing, he loathes it.
  他岂止不喜欢，简直讨厌唱歌。
  Your account is far from (being) true.
  你所说远非事实。

11. be sure of oneself： 对自己有把握，自信
  He is always sure of himself whatever he does.
  无论做什么，他总是对自己充满自信。
  Fight no battle you are not sure of winning.
  不打无把握之仗。

12. take it into one's head that ...： 毫无根据地认为……
  He took it into his head that he had been there before.
  他毫无根据地认为他以前去过那里。
  She took it into her head to go to the mountains alone.
  她心血来潮，突然想独自一人去爬山。

13. begin with： 首先
  Let's begin with Lesson One.
  我们从第一课开始吧。
  Knowledge begin with practice.
  认识从实践开始。

14. not without some pride： 不无骄傲地
  He spoke plausibly, not without foundation.
  他振振有辞，不无根据。

15. go on to do something： 继续做某事
  注意 go on to do something 与 go on doing something 的区别：前者是继续做另外一件事；
  后者是继续做前面提到的那件事。
  After dinner, I went on to help him with his homework.
  晚饭过后，我继续辅导他做功课。
  Don't go on talking about this matter.
  不要继续谈这件事了。

16. That was too much for the Emperor.
  那对皇帝来讲太过份了。
  The job is too much for her.
  这工作对她来说太难了。
  You talk too much.
  你说得太多了。

17. That beats me.
  这可真把我难住了。
  It beats me; I don't know the answer.
  这可难倒了我，我不知道答案。
  That problem has beaten him.
  那个问题把他难住了。

18. either ... or ...： 不是……就是……
  Either we go now or we remain here forever.
  要么我们现在走，要么我们永远在这待下去。
  I want to buy my daughter a birthday present, either a dictionary or an encyclopedia.
  我要给我女儿买一样生日礼物，不是词典就是百科全书。
  Either you or she is wrong.
  不是你错了，就是她错了。

19. your majesty： 陛下（您）
  Her Majesty the Queen：女王陛下（她）
  His Majesty the Prince：王子殿下（他）

20. forget to do something： 忘记做某事
  注意 forget to do something 与 forget doing something 的区别：前者是“忘记做某事”，
  即事情还没有做；后者是“忘记做过某事”。
  I forgot to post the letter.
  我忘记寄这封信了。
  I shall never forget hearing her singing that song.
  我永远不会忘记听她唱那支歌时的情景。

21. walk past： 路过
  Days went past without any news.
  时日过去，消息全无。
  The soldiers filed past the general.
  士兵们从将军身边鱼贯而过。
